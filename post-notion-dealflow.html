Notion Deal Flow Tracker

Published: Sep 1, 2025 • Reading time: ~3–4 min

Overview

The Notion Deal Flow Tracker is a lightweight tool designed to mirror the way investment professionals structure and monitor opportunities. It combines a clear tabular layout with calculated fields for key metrics (MoM, IRR) and offers different views (table, board, gallery) to adapt to the deal pipeline stage.

Its main purpose is to bring structure to a noisy process: opportunities can be logged consistently, assumptions are recorded explicitly, and outcomes can be compared across deals. By integrating simple formulas, the tracker helps transform raw entries into actionable insights while remaining easy to maintain.

This overview highlights the core philosophy of the template: clarity, consistency, and decision-making support. In the following sections, we will dive into its structure, key fields, and examples of how it can be used in practice.

Template Structure & Key Fields

The tracker captures both qualitative and quantitative aspects of each deal, ensuring that opportunities are logged with enough context to support investment decisions. Each row corresponds to a company under evaluation, with fields structured as follows:

  • Core Identifiers: Company, Sector, Country, and Deal Stage provide the basic classification and pipeline position.
  • Valuation Metrics: Valuation (€M), EBITDA (€M), and EV/EBITDA Multiple frame the financial baseline of the opportunity.
  • Deal Economics: Deal Size (€M), MoM Multiple, and Holding Period (Years) quantify the potential commitment and timeframe.
  • Returns Analysis: IRR (Est.) is calculated directly, complemented by an Expected IRR (%) column for quick reference and comparison.
  • Ownership & Tracking: Each deal has an assigned Owner, Notes for qualitative observations, and a Last Contact Date to ensure timely follow-ups.
  • Size Classification: A Deal Size Check field automatically tags transactions as Small, Mid, or Large, adding a quick-scan dimension to the pipeline.

This is the first version of the template. An updated Excel version with additional features will be available soon. If you have any suggestions, feel free to contact me via email or connect with me on LinkedIn.
