Hot CPI, Green Equities: Why Risk Didn’t Panic

Inflation was higher than expected — normally that kills stocks. But today equities pushed up instead of selling off. Here's how traders explained the move on desks this morning.

31 Oct 2025 • 3 min read • by Stefano Campita

CPI came in hotter than expected on both headline and core. Usually that’s an instant “risk off” trigger: yields spike, growth names get hit, banks smile. But instead, equities opened flat and then drifted higher.

The desk narrative was simple: yes inflation is sticky, but no one believes the Fed wants to blow up growth to kill the last 0.5% of it. In other words, the market is betting on a “soft landing” story, not a “Volcker 2.0” story. As long as that story survives, dips get bought.

1. Rates are high, but panic is low

Treasury yields moved, but not violently. The move looked more like “repricing the path of cuts” and less like “oh no, regime change.” That matters. When bonds don’t explode, equities don’t have to puke.

The signal: traders are accepting slower disinflation without fully pricing a recession. That’s bullish for risk assets in the short term, but fragile if labor data weakens.

2. Tech didn’t crack, and that sets the mood

If mega-cap tech holds, the index holds. It’s that simple. Today big-cap tech traded like “we can handle slightly higher-for-longer rates.” As long as the earnings story is intact, macro fear doesn’t fully control the tape.

3. What this means for you

For interviews and stock pitches, the line to remember is: “Markets are still pricing a soft landing. Inflation is annoying, not catastrophic. Positioning is defensive, not panicked.”
