LBO Modelling: From Assumptions to IRR

A Leveraged Buyout (LBO) is one of the most iconic transactions in the world of finance: acquiring a company through a combination of debt and equity. The purpose of this section is to provide a step-by-step walkthrough of how I build a complete LBO model, starting from the base assumptions and progressing all the way to the calculation of the internal rate of return (IRR).

Starting Assumptions

These are the starting assumptions given (I didn't calculate anything here).

Revenues (LTM): 100 M$

Annual Revenue Growth: 10% (constant for the 5 years)

EBITDA (LTM): 50 M$

EBITDA Margin: constant

D&A + Capex: 5% of annual revenues

NWC: unchanged

Tax Rate: 25%

Entry Multiple: 10X EBITDA

Exit Multiple: 10X EBITDA after 5 years

Projection Period: 5 Years

Initial Leverage: 6X EBITDA

Debt Interest Rate: 8%

No capital amortization until maturity

TEV & Capital Structure

Now is the time to start some calculations. Here I calculated the purchase TEV and I determined the initial capital structure, divided in Debt & Equity.

Step 1 = Purchase TEV

EBITDA LTM = 50

Entry Multiple = 10X

TEV_in = 50 x 10 = 500

Step 2 = Capital Structure

From assumption the Initial Leverage is 6X EBITDA

Debt = 6 x 50 = 300

Equity = 500 - 300 = 200

Result

Tev_in = 500

initial Debt = 300 (60%)

Equity Sponsor = 200 (40%)

Interest expense on Debt

I calculated the interest expense on the debt keeping in mind that is not being paid down through the period (5 years). with this being said, i understood that the interest expense will be the same every year.

Debt & Tax Rates

Total Debt = 300

Interest Rate = 8%

Annual Interest Expense = 300 x 8% = 24

Projection over 5 years

In My opinion the following part is the most fascinating one. We are projeting P&L down to LFCF over the 5 years.

Exit TEV & Returns

Now for the closing part of this LBO Model I calculated the Exit TEV, Mom (Multiple of Money) and IRR (Internal Rate of Return)

Step 1 = Exit TEV

EBITDA Y5 = 80.5

Exit Multiple = 10X

TEV_out = 80.5 x 10 = 805

Step 2 = Exit Equity Value

TEV_out = 805

Net Debt = 300

Equity Exit Value = 805 - 300 = 505

Step 3 = Returns

Invested Equityb> = 200

Exit Equity = 505 (60%)

Mom = 505/200 = 2.53%

I calculated the IRR by intuition:

if 2x in 5 years = 15%

2x in 3 years = 25%

3x in 5 years = 25%

3x in 3 years = 45%

Therefore 2.5x in 5 years is about 20% IRR

Pay attention! In this setup, the debt is structured as bullet repayment, meaning it is not amortized during the holding period: the company pays only interest each year and repays the full principal at exit. As a result, Free Cash Flows do not reduce the outstanding debt, which remains constant at 300 until year five.

This LBO model illustrates how assumptions on growth, leverage, and exit multiples translate into investor returns, delivering ~20% IRR and a 2.5x MoM over five years
